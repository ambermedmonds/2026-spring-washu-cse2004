Club Discovery Platform

Context ART 332J: UX Research Methods in Design
Timeline 8 Weeks (Feb 2025 - Apr 2025)
Skills UX Research · Prototyping · User Testing
Software Figma


Background

In my UX Research Methods for Design course, I led a full UX research and strategy process focused on improving how WashU students discover and join campus organizations, while also making it easier for club leaders to recruit, onboard, and engage new members. I translated research insights into a prototype designed to centralize club information, personalize discovery, and streamline onboarding for both students and club leadership.


Challenge

Club discovery at WashU is fragmented across word-of-mouth, social media, flyers, and an existing platform called WUGO. Information is often outdated or incomplete, making it difficult for students to compare options, understand expectations, or take the next step. Meanwhile, club leaders struggle with engaging new members as well as time-consuming onboarding and administrative workflows.


Objective

Understand the challenges WashU students face in finding and joining student organizations, as well as the difficulties club leaders encounter in recruiting and onboarding new members. Then, identify key pain points and explore opportunities to improve both club discovery and member engagement.


My Approach

  • Surveys & Interviews: uncovered student motivations, behaviors, and decision-making habits
  • Secondary Research: benchmarked how existing tools support student org discovery
  • Affinity Mapping: synthesized patterns into clear themes and insights
  • User Personas: captured distinct user types and needs to guide design decisions
  • User Journey Mapping: identified pain points across discovery, evaluation, and onboarding
  • Prototyping: explored an end-to-end concept to bring the solution to life
  • Usability Testing: refined navigation, content structure, and information hierarchy
